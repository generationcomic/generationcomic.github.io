After a four year hiatus, the Australian comic anthology Generation is returning. This project is still in its early stages, but in the coming months we will be inviting artists and opening up submissions for short comic stories, with the goal to publish a collected volume in early 2014.

About

Generation was founded in 2004 by Sydney illustrator Katie Huang and other local artists who wanted to produce their own professional level publication. The success of the first issue grew into a yearly offering of short stories from Australia’s burgeoning manga and comics community. Although Generation ceased its original publication in 2009, it hasn’t been forgotten as an inspiration and showcase for Australian talent.

There is an amazing community of artists here in Australia, and with the rise of social media and crowdfunding, the way we connect and share stories, art and information has changed a lot in the last four years. It’s important that we don’t lose those things that help inspire and grow our communities.

Submit

Generation accepts professional level short story submissions of up to 16 pages. Black and white comics preferred, but colour submissions can be negotiated. Stand alone illustrations are also accepted (with a preference for illustrations of scenes or narratives)

For more information please see our submission guidelines.

Support

One of the best ways you can support us right now is to spread the word about Generation! Follow us on social media and let your comic fans and artist friends know what we’re all about.

When the book is ready we will most likely be running a campaign on Pozible to help fund the printing costs as well (Why Pozible and not Kickstarter? Well, only the cool kids in America and England get to use Kickstarter, that’s why).

About the Editor

Nadia Attlee is a Sydney based graphic designer and illustrator who has contributed to two past issues of Generation. She is also a founding member of the Sydney Manga and Anime Show, where she helmed the Design Department as well as being the 2009-2011 Vice President. She has experience in print design as well as having worked for over a year at Animal Logic on the Warner Bros' LEGO feature film.

Now that she has all her free time back from not being involved in SMASH!, she continues to pursue her love of comics and playing too much Assassin’s Creed.
